## 01 Background

- Generative AI entered education and workplaces rapidly after 2022.
- China expected 12.22 million college graduates in 2025.
- STEM graduates traditionally earn higher returns from technical, data, and engineering skills.
- Social science work is increasingly AI-assisted across marketing, finance, consulting, HR, media, and policy.

**Key tension:** Is AI mainly a complement to STEM skills, or a general-purpose tool that benefits many majors?

## 02 Research Question(s)

**Main question:** Has generative AI widened or narrowed the wage gap between STEM and social science graduates in China?

1. Do technology-intensive occupations offer higher wages?
2. Are STEM majors still concentrated in high-paying early-career jobs?
3. Can social science graduates gain from AI-enhanced productivity?

**H1:** If AI complements technical skills, the STEM wage premium increases.  
**H2:** If AI lowers barriers to analytical work, the major-based wage gap may decrease.

## 03 Method

**Data sources:** NBS wage data; MOE graduate counts; CGSS, CFPS, and CHFS surveys; online job postings; MyCOS employment reports.

**Variables:** monthly and annual wage; log wage; STEM wage premium; major type; post-2022 period; AI keywords; occupation and demographic controls.

**Strategy:** collect data -> classify majors -> tag AI exposure -> compare wages -> interpret the change in the STEM premium.

`log(Wage) = beta0 + beta1 STEM + beta2 PostAI + beta3 STEM x PostAI + Controls + epsilon`

## 04 Results

- China expected 12.22 million college graduates in 2025.
- Professional and technical workers earned CNY 148,046 annually, above both urban sector averages.
- Selected high-paying early-career majors are concentrated in engineering and information technology.
- ChatGPT-related skill demand may diffuse from 28% of occupations currently to as much as 45%.

**Key findings:** Technical skills retain a strong premium; AI may widen the short-run STEM advantage; AI diffusion creates a route for non-STEM productivity gains.

## 05 Discussion & Conclusion

- AI changes the value of skills rather than simply replacing workers.
- STEM graduates benefit when AI complements programming, engineering, and data analysis.
- Social science graduates benefit when domain knowledge is paired with AI literacy.

**Main takeaway:** AI rewards workers who combine technical tools with human judgment.

## 06 Evaluation

**Strengths:** multiple evidence sources; timely question; direct comparison of major groups.

**Limitations:** statistics are rarely organized by major; AI use is imperfectly measured; postings are not received wages.

**Future work:** estimate occupation-specific AI premiums and survey actual AI use among graduates.
